# Give picture and sound the same motion path

> Tora3 uses object trajectories as a shared kinematic control for video and audio, tying visible movement and acoustic events to the same timed structure.

Most trajectory-conditioned systems treat the path as a visual constraint, leaving sound to infer timing from pixels or text. Tora3 propagates first-frame visual features along the requested trajectory and derives second-order kinematic states from that path for its audio-alignment module. A hybrid flow-matching objective reinforces the conditioned region without forcing the whole frame onto the trajectory.

The authors also assemble PAV, a dataset emphasizing motion-relevant audiovisual patterns with automatically extracted motion annotations. Their experiments report improvements over open baselines in motion realism, motion-sound synchronization, and overall audiovisual quality. The framework is presently research evidence rather than a released Tora3 production tool; the public Tora repository announces the paper but exposes earlier Tora model assets.

## Why this was selected

It offers a legible control signal for a specific failure in generated audiovisual work: sound that recognizes the object but misses the action.
