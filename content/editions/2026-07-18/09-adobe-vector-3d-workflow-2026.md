# After Effects keeps imported vectors editable

> Adobe's June update lets After Effects import SVGs as editable shape layers and adds object-aware rotoscoping, surface displacement, depth of field, and scripted parametric meshes.

Direct SVG import preserves gradients, strokes, and transparency instead of flattening the artwork or requiring an intermediate conversion. A related Illustrator copy-and-paste path moves vector content into After Effects as shapes. For motion systems built from designed assets, that keeps geometry and styling available for animation after handoff.

Object Matte replaces the brush-only Roto Brush workflow with object selection, quick selection, a selection brush, and refine-edge tools. The same update adds displacement maps and depth of field for 3D models, meshes, text, and shape layers, plus scripting access to parametric meshes. These are rolling product features, so availability should be checked in the installed build before restructuring a production file around them.

## Why this was selected

Editable SVG handoff and scriptable mesh generation remove ordinary friction from a real motion-design pipeline without requiring a new generative workflow.
