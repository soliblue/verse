# Treat liquid particles as samples in space and time

> ST-FLIP jitters particles along the time axis as well as in space, suppressing temporal aliasing so liquid solvers can take much larger steps without destroying flow structure.

In FLIP-style simulation, particles that travel too far during one step undersample their paths and can produce projection artifacts. ST-FLIP interprets each particle as a four-dimensional space-time sample, then deposits it through a separable 4D kernel into slab-integrated 3D grid fields.

The extension needs one additional particle attribute, no extra grids, and no extra linear solve. The authors report two-to-eight-times speedups in their tests, including multi-billion-particle scenes, while preserving coherent free surfaces and two-phase flows at Courant numbers beyond normal practice. It is designed to plug into FLIP, PIC, and APIC solvers rather than replace them.

## Why this was selected

The anti-aliasing interpretation is simple, transferable, and tied to a substantial production bottleneck in large fluid effects.
