# SoundStager turns a video narrative into an editable soundscape

> Adobe Research's CHI 2026 system proposes layered generative soundscapes that remain editable through conversational and direct controls instead of collapsing everything into one audio result.

SoundStager starts from a familiar failure in generative audio: a creator can ask for a sound, but often cannot describe it precisely or regain control once several sounds arrive as a premixed result. The system analyzes a video's narrative and proposes layered scenes using four soundscape roles: keynote sounds, signals, soundmarks, and archetypal sounds. Creators then refine the result with both conversational and direct controls.

The interface was informed by studies with six professional sound designers and six video creators, followed by an evaluation with twelve video creators. The reported result is not simply faster generation; it is a workflow that preserves iterative control. The reusable technique is to separate narrative function from sonic material. Decide what a layer does in the scene, keep it as a stem, and only then tune its source, placement, and mix.

## Why this was selected

It offers a usable vocabulary and interaction pattern for generative sound design, not only a new model or a polished demo.
