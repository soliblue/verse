# At silent green, archives become glitches, loops, code, and sound

> The Transgressive Memory Festival takes place September 12 and 13, 2026 at silent green in Berlin, bringing media art, performance, discussion, and exhibition work to the politics of digital memory.

Körber-Stiftung's eCommemoration Festival frames memory as something actively reconstructed by bodies, databases, networks, and algorithms. Its 2026 edition brings media art, performance, critical discussion, and an exhibition to silent green on September 12 and 13. The announced theme focuses on work that uses digital grammars such as glitches, loops, archives, image, code, and sound to unsettle linear historical narratives.

The detailed program is still to be announced, so this is a calendar hold rather than a ticket recommendation. Its value is the framing: audiovisual technique is treated as a way of organizing evidence and public memory, not only as surface. It also overlaps Berlin Art Week, making that weekend unusually dense for research across institutional, independent, and media-art contexts.

## Why this was selected

The festival connects audiovisual form to archives and public memory, and its overlap with Berlin Art Week makes the weekend efficient to reserve now.
