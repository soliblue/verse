# Draw the instruction directly over the thing that should move

> Notational Animating turns sketched paths, forces, poses, and targets into keyframes, then keeps ambiguous parts editable through dynamic controls and visual feedback.

The researchers analyzed 135 real animation sketches and found that motion notations are contextual, combinational, and often ambiguous. They formalized a notation as source, path, and target, giving the system a structured interpretation before it generates animation.

The prototype overlays high-level marks on a static drawing, converts them into intended motion, and exposes parameters through dynamic interface widgets. A closed feedback loop lets the animator correct the interpretation rather than accept a one-shot result. Adobe lists the CHI 2026 work as a best paper honorable mention.

## Why this was selected

It preserves a familiar animation gesture while using automation only where the notation leaves room for interpretation.
