# Map a song's emotional arc into a 360-degree scene

> Bring Music The Horizon estimates valence and arousal every four bars, converts that trajectory into controllable keyframe guidance, and expands the results into continuous 360-degree video.

The pipeline first derives a low-frequency emotional structure from the song. EmotiCrafter turns that structure into visual guidance, while SEGA lets the creator alter semantic directions before image-to-video models connect the keyframes.

The project demonstrates the method across several musical genres and provides qualitative comparisons with an audio-to-visual baseline. It does not establish a quantitative advantage. The useful compositional move is narrower: make the time scale of musical analysis explicit, then keep emotional guidance editable before generating motion.

## Why this was selected

It offers a concrete bridge from musical form to immersive image sequencing without pretending that raw beat reactivity is enough.
