# Article guidance

Choose work that gives the reader a concrete idea, release, method, or finding. Skip generic trend pieces, repeated announcements, weak aggregation, and items whose relevance needs inflated language.

Each article needs a one- or two-sentence quoted summary, two to four short factual paragraphs, and one short selection reason tied to the preferences. Use `paper` or `technique` for `kind`, `nightjar-articles-v1` as `prompt_version`, and record the research time.

Retain original publication timestamps when known. More sources do not make a weak story stronger.
